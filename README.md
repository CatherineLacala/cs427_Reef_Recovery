**Tools used:** Unity, Adobe Illustrator, Maya, C#\
\
This original team project was created for our CS427 Creative Coding class.\
Within this course, we spent the entire semester creating a 3D VR experience using the 2019 Unity version to be developed and exhibited in the CAVE2 Virtual Reality Environment at the Electronic Visualization Laboratory (EVL), at the University of Illinois Chicago.\
\
**GOAL:** Our project aims to visualize coral reef health as a dynamic system affected by human intervention. Instead of focusing solely on reef destruction, the project has users apply different restoration strategies which affect the reef’s appearance.

This project helps advance research because it translates complicated marine ecology into experiential understanding as well as providing insight to how people understand ecosystem resilience when changes are experienced. It also supports previous research about how immersive visualizations improve comprehension of long-term environmental processes

Reef Recovery contributes to the Sustainable Development Goal #14: Life Below Water, where it shows cause-and-effect relationships between human behavior and reef health.

### 🔉 Recovery Method 
By integrating interactive design, visual storytelling, and environmental immersion, the project engages users as active contributors in ecological restoration. Recent biological discoveries have identified acoustic enrichment as a viable catalyst for Reef Recovery. The Reef Acoustic Playback System (RAPS) is a self-contained, solar-powered device designed to facilitate coral reef restoration through broadcasting the sound cues of a vibrant, healthy reef into degraded ocean habitats.\
\
Users are a member of the "Reef Recovery Diving Team," where they begin on land, setting up solar panels to generate power underwater for speaker soundsystems. After that, the user dives into the water and swims down to a dead coral reef. There, they will find the Reef Quarters, where "SPEAKER" typography is located. The user will pick up "SPEAKER" and place them in the pre-designated areas within the reef to install a speaker. Once placed, the user can increase or decrease the acoustic sounds played on the speaker. Based on the given location of the speaker, coral typography will begin to grow around the speaker's location as well as fish typography spawns in.
